# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Investment professionals: CFA charterholders, quants, portfolio analysts at mid-market firms, plus academics and students at partner universities. They configure a financial data pull (providers, date ranges, tickers, output columns) at a desk during the workday, then run it and wait minutes for results. Expert users, low tolerance for friction or decoration; they live in Excel, terminals, and research notebooks.

## Product Purpose

Data Curator retrieves, validates, and combines market and fundamental data into reproducible, auditable datasets — the front door of the KaxaNuk Research Lab suite. The parameters panel is the zero-code path: scaffold a workspace, edit a JSON config visually, run the pipeline, read the output log. Success = a user configures and launches a correct run in under a minute without reading docs.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, pragmatic, decisive. Board-room-ready but engineer-built. Quiet confidence: the tool states facts (what ran, what failed, where output went) without hype or hand-holding.

## Anti-references

- Crypto-dashboard neon: glow effects, saturated gradients on dark.
- SaaS marketing gloss: hero metrics, gradient text, decorative illustration.
- Bloomberg-terminal cosplay: dense for density's sake, all-caps everywhere.
- Generic admin-template look: identical card grids, side-stripe alert borders.

## Design Principles

- The tool disappears into the task: standard affordances, no invented controls.
- State is always visible and truthful: a run is idle, running, finished, or failed — say which, plainly.
- Density where data lives (ticker chips, column picker), air where decisions happen (general parameters, run button).
- Errors are first-class copy: what failed, where, what to do next.
- Earned familiarity over novelty: a Linear/Stripe-fluent user should trust every control on sight.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard-reachable controls with visible focus; WCAG AA contrast on both light and dark schemes (panel uses light-dark()); no information conveyed by color alone; respects prefers-reduced-motion (motion is minimal anyway).
